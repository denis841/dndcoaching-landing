# Landing Page Conversion Checklist
## For Service-Based Businesses & High-Ticket Coaching Offers

Use this checklist when **building** a new landing page or **auditing** an existing one. Each item is derived from research on pages converting at 5%+ for coaching and service businesses.

**Scoring Guide:**
- ✅ = Implemented well
- ⚠️ = Partially done / needs improvement
- ❌ = Missing entirely

---

## Section 1: Above-The-Fold (First Screen)

*Users form opinions in 0.05 seconds. This section determines if they stay or leave.*

### Headline
- [ ] Leads with **transformation**, not description
- [ ] Addresses the prospect's core desire or frustration
- [ ] Avoids generic language ("coaching program," "get fit," etc.)
- [ ] Under 10 words for primary headline
- [ ] Would make sense to someone who knows nothing about you

**Test:** Read your headline aloud. Does it answer "What will change in my life?"

### Subheadline
- [ ] Expands on the headline with specificity
- [ ] Includes timeframe, method, or unique mechanism
- [ ] Hints at your differentiator (methodology, guarantee, etc.)
- [ ] 1-2 sentences maximum

### Visual Elements
- [ ] High-quality image of you (the service provider) visible
- [ ] Image shows you as approachable and professional
- [ ] If using video: thumbnail with play button (no auto-play)
- [ ] No stock photos of random people
- [ ] Visual hierarchy guides eye to headline → subheadline → CTA

### Primary CTA
- [ ] Visible without scrolling on desktop AND mobile
- [ ] Button text is action-oriented and first-person ("See If I Qualify")
- [ ] Contrasting color that stands out from page
- [ ] Single, clear action (not multiple competing CTAs)
- [ ] Frames application as selective, not transactional

### Trust Signal (Optional but Recommended)
- [ ] Quick credibility marker present (client count, years experience, or mini-testimonial)
- [ ] Placed near CTA to reduce friction
- [ ] Specific numbers beat vague claims ("127 clients transformed" > "many satisfied clients")

---

## Section 2: Problem Identification

*Make them feel understood before offering solutions.*

### Pain Point Articulation
- [ ] Names their frustration in their words (not industry jargon)
- [ ] Describes the **symptoms** they're experiencing
- [ ] Acknowledges what they've already tried
- [ ] Validates that the problem isn't their fault (willpower, laziness, etc.)
- [ ] Creates "how did they know?" moment

### Emotional Connection
- [ ] Uses "you" language throughout
- [ ] Specific scenarios they'll recognize from their life
- [ ] Avoids making them feel stupid or attacked
- [ ] Tone is empathetic, not preachy

**Test:** Would your ideal client read this and say "That's exactly how I feel"?

---

## Section 3: Solution Introduction

*Bridge from their problem to your unique approach.*

### Methodology Positioning
- [ ] Names your framework/method (proprietary name increases perceived value)
- [ ] Explains WHY your approach is different (not just what it is)
- [ ] Connects directly to why previous solutions failed them
- [ ] Simple enough to understand in 30 seconds
- [ ] Positions you as guide, not hero

### Differentiator Visibility
- [ ] Main USP clearly stated (not buried)
- [ ] Explains the "mechanism" that makes results possible
- [ ] Avoids generic claims everyone makes ("personalized," "comprehensive")
- [ ] Answers "Why should I believe this will work when nothing else did?"

---

## Section 4: Transformation Bridge

*Paint the before/after picture vividly.*

### Future State Description
- [ ] Specific, tangible outcomes described
- [ ] Uses "from... to..." or "imagine..." language
- [ ] Includes both external results AND internal feelings
- [ ] Realistic but compelling (not overpromising)
- [ ] Connects to what they actually want (may not be the obvious thing)

### Timeline Clarity
- [ ] Clear timeframe for results
- [ ] Milestone markers if applicable ("By week 4, you'll...")
- [ ] Sets appropriate expectations

---

## Section 5: Social Proof (First Placement)

*Proof that your promise is real.*

### Testimonial Quality
- [ ] 2-3 powerful testimonials in this section
- [ ] Testimonials feature people **like your target client**
- [ ] Includes specific outcomes/numbers ("lost 27 lbs" not "lost weight")
- [ ] Uses Before-After-Experience (BAE) format
- [ ] Real names and photos included (102.5% conversion lift)

### Testimonial Format
- [ ] Video testimonials if available (highest trust)
- [ ] Written testimonials include client photo
- [ ] Each testimonial addresses a different objection/concern
- [ ] No obviously fake or vague testimonials

**Test:** Would a skeptical prospect believe these are real people?

---

## Section 6: How It Works

*Reduce overwhelm with clear process.*

### Process Clarity
- [ ] 3-5 step process maximum
- [ ] Each step has clear name and brief description
- [ ] Steps are outcome-focused, not task-focused
- [ ] Visual treatment (numbered, icons, timeline)
- [ ] Makes the journey feel manageable

### Next Step Clarity
- [ ] Clear what happens immediately after they apply/sign up
- [ ] Removes uncertainty about "what do I do next?"

---

## Section 7: About You / Coach Introduction

*Build the relationship and trust.*

### Personal Story
- [ ] Connects your story to their struggle
- [ ] Explains WHY you do this work
- [ ] Shows you understand because you've been there (or worked with those who have)
- [ ] Authentic and human (not corporate)
- [ ] Appropriate length (not your full biography)

### Credibility Markers
- [ ] Relevant credentials/certifications mentioned
- [ ] Experience quantified (years, clients, results)
- [ ] Credentials introduced through context, not bragging
- [ ] Photo of you (ideally different from hero section)

---

## Section 8: Who This Is For / Not For

*Pre-qualification builds trust.*

### "This Is For You If..." Section
- [ ] 4-6 specific criteria listed
- [ ] Describes situations, not demographics
- [ ] Ideal client sees themselves clearly
- [ ] Focuses on mindset and readiness, not just circumstances

### "This Is NOT For You If..." Section
- [ ] Honest about who won't benefit
- [ ] Filters out poor-fit clients
- [ ] Shows you care about results, not just sales
- [ ] Specific enough to actually disqualify some people

**Test:** Would someone reading this clearly know if they qualify or not?

---

## Section 9: Full Program/Service Breakdown

*Stack value strategically.*

### Deliverables Presentation
- [ ] Benefits-focused, not feature-focused
- [ ] Each component explains the "so that..." outcome
- [ ] Organized logically (not overwhelming list)
- [ ] Visual hierarchy emphasizes most valuable elements
- [ ] Includes everything they get (calls, resources, support, etc.)

### Value Communication
- [ ] Total value is clear (implicit or explicit)
- [ ] Price positioned against transformation value, not competitors
- [ ] ROI frame used ("less than $X per day for...")

---

## Section 10: Guarantee Section

*Remove the risk.*

### Guarantee Visibility
- [ ] Dedicated section (not buried in fine print)
- [ ] Visual treatment (badge, seal, box)
- [ ] Placed near pricing information
- [ ] Prominent enough to notice while scanning

### Guarantee Clarity
- [ ] Terms clearly explained
- [ ] Conditions stated positively (what they need to do)
- [ ] Explains WHY you can offer this (track record)
- [ ] Addresses their core fear directly

### Guarantee Strength
- [ ] Actually meaningful (not weaselly or full of loopholes)
- [ ] Risk genuinely reversed to you
- [ ] Demonstrates confidence in your methodology

---

## Section 11: Pricing Presentation

*If showing price on page.*

### Price Positioning
- [ ] Transformation value established BEFORE price shown
- [ ] Price compared to cost of NOT solving the problem
- [ ] Payment options if available (payment plan, etc.)
- [ ] No apology for price

### Price Anchoring
- [ ] Value stack or comparison used appropriately
- [ ] ROI calculation if relevant
- [ ] Investment frame, not cost frame

---

## Section 12: FAQ Section

*Handle objections proactively.*

### Question Selection
- [ ] Addresses top 5-7 objections/concerns
- [ ] Includes "Will this work for me?" variation
- [ ] Addresses time/commitment concerns
- [ ] Addresses previous failure concerns
- [ ] Includes pricing/payment questions

### Answer Quality
- [ ] Answers are helpful, not defensive
- [ ] Specific and direct (not evasive)
- [ ] Reframes objections as benefits where appropriate
- [ ] Links to more info or CTA where relevant

---

## Section 13: Final CTA Section

*Close with clarity and momentum.*

### Call to Action
- [ ] Transformation promise restated
- [ ] Clear, single action to take
- [ ] Button prominent and compelling
- [ ] Any genuine urgency mentioned (cohort dates, limited spots)

### Urgency (If Used)
- [ ] Urgency is REAL, not fabricated
- [ ] Explained why urgency exists (capacity, dates, bonuses)
- [ ] No fake countdown timers or scarcity

---

## Section 14: Technical & UX Requirements

*The invisible conversion killers.*

### Page Speed
- [ ] Loads in under 3 seconds (test: Google PageSpeed Insights)
- [ ] Images compressed and optimized
- [ ] No auto-playing videos
- [ ] Minimal third-party scripts

### Mobile Experience
- [ ] Fully responsive design
- [ ] CTA visible without scrolling on mobile
- [ ] Touch-friendly buttons (minimum 44x44 pixels)
- [ ] Font size minimum 16px
- [ ] Forms easy to complete on phone
- [ ] Single-column layout on mobile

### Navigation
- [ ] Minimal or no navigation menu (reduces exits)
- [ ] No competing CTAs or links
- [ ] Single focus: get them to apply/book

### Form Optimization
- [ ] Minimum necessary fields only
- [ ] Multi-step form for longer applications
- [ ] Progress indicator for multi-step
- [ ] Mobile-optimized input types (email keyboard, number pad, etc.)
- [ ] Clear error messages
- [ ] Submit button text is specific ("Submit My Application")

---

## Section 15: Application/Intake Process

*For high-ticket offers with qualification.*

### Application Positioning
- [ ] Framed as "mutual fit assessment," not form to fill
- [ ] Language emphasizes selectivity ("See if we're a good fit")
- [ ] Application process itself provides value (clarity, reflection)
- [ ] Sets expectation for response time

### Application Questions
- [ ] 5-7 qualifying questions maximum for initial form
- [ ] Questions serve dual purpose (qualification + prospect clarity)
- [ ] Includes commitment/investment readiness question
- [ ] Open-ended question captures pain/motivation in their words
- [ ] Progressive disclosure (basic info first, deeper questions later)

### Post-Application Experience
- [ ] Immediate confirmation (thank you page or email)
- [ ] Clear next step communicated
- [ ] Timeline for response stated
- [ ] Value delivered even before call (PDF, mini-insight, etc.)

---

## Section 16: Discovery Call Setup

*For funnels that include a call.*

### Call Positioning
- [ ] Positioned as "strategy session" or "clarity call," not sales call
- [ ] Promise of value regardless of outcome
- [ ] Length specified (25-45 minutes typical)
- [ ] What will be covered is clear

### Pre-Call Communication
- [ ] Confirmation email with prep instructions
- [ ] Reminder sequence (24hr, 1hr before)
- [ ] Easy to reschedule if needed
- [ ] Reduces no-shows through investment of time/prep

## Implementation Notes

**When Building New:**
- Work through sections 1-13 in order
- Don't skip the Problem section (most coaches undersell this)
- Write more than you need, then cut
- Get the structure right before perfecting copy

**When Auditing Existing:**
- Score each item honestly
- Screenshot your page and annotate gaps
- Prioritize fixes by section importance (above)
- Test changes one at a time when possible

**What Matters Most:**
1. Above-the-fold clarity and CTA visibility
2. Mobile experience (majority of traffic)
3. Page speed (under 3 seconds)
4. Specific social proof from similar clients
5. Guarantee visibility and strength
6. Application/CTA positioning as selective
